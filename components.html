Component Recommendations

The picker tells you which CPU, GPU, RAM, and storage to buy. This page covers everything else: case, motherboard, cooler, PSU, and RAM brand. Updated March 2026.

Case
Fractal Design North
~$130
Mid-tower ATX. Walnut or oak wood slats, mesh front, excellent airflow. Fits GPUs up to 355mm. Two 140mm fans included. The prettiest case in production -- looks right in an architecture office. The Momentum Edition ($179) is quieter with three 120mm fans if you can find it.
Fractal Design North XL
~$180
E-ATX support, 420mm front radiator, fits the largest GPUs with room to spare (413mm clearance). Same aesthetic as the North, sized up. The Momentum XL Edition ($209) adds rear-connector motherboard support and quieter fans. Get this if you need E-ATX or a 5090-class card.
or
Asus ProArt PA602
~$270 (Wood Edition ~$300)
E-ATX, 420mm top radiator, more drive bays than the Fractal. IR dust indicator, power lock latch, tool-less PCIe mounts. The Wood Edition has wooden side panels that match the ProArt motherboard aesthetic. Bigger and heavier than the North XL but more expansion room.
Motherboard
Intel Arrow Lake (LGA 1851)
For the Ultra 5 245K, Ultra 7 265K, or Ultra 9 285K.
Asus ProArt Z890-Creator WiFi
~$420
Thunderbolt 4, 10G LAN, excellent VRM. The natural pair for a ProArt case. The Creator boards have ports that matter for architecture: Thunderbolt for external storage and docks, multiple M.2 slots, reliable BIOS.
or
MSI MAG Z890 Tomahawk WiFi
~$260
Strong VRM, WiFi 7, good I/O. Less premium than the ProArt but does the job. No Thunderbolt -- if you need TB4, go ProArt or add a discrete card.
AMD AM5
For the Ryzen 7 9800X3D, Ryzen 9 9900X, or Ryzen 9 9950X. AM5 has a longer confirmed upgrade runway than Intel -- support through at least 2027, possibly longer.
Asus ProArt X870E-Creator WiFi
~$450
Thunderbolt 4, dual 10G+2.5G LAN, USB4. The AMD equivalent of the Z890 ProArt Creator.
or
MSI MAG X870 Tomahawk WiFi
~$270
Solid VRM, WiFi 7, good value. Same story as the Intel Tomahawk: capable and affordable, no Thunderbolt.
Budget boards (B650/B760 class): Fine for the Ultra 5 245K or Ryzen 7 9700X in a review/drafting build. Not recommended for the 285K, 9950X, or any build with a high-end GPU -- VRM and power delivery matter under sustained load.
Memory (DDR5)
Corsair Vengeance DDR5-6000 CL30
~$90 (32 GB) / ~$180 (64 GB) / ~$360 (128 GB)
Reliable, widely compatible, reasonable price. DDR5-6000 at CL30 is the sweet spot for both Intel and AMD platforms.
or
G.Skill Trident Z5 DDR5-6000 CL30
Similar pricing
Slightly better binning in some kits, excellent QVL support. Either brand is fine. Pick whichever is cheaper.
RAM prices are rising in early 2026 due to the DRAM shortage. 64 GB kits that were $120 in late 2025 are now $180+. 32 GB is fine for small projects. 64 GB covers most BIM modeling. 128 GB only if you regularly have 500MB+ linked models or run V-Ray production renders.
Storage (NVMe SSD)
WD Black SN850X
~$80 (1 TB) / ~$150 (2 TB) / ~$300 (4 TB)
PCIe Gen 4, fast sequential and random I/O. Gen 4 is all you need -- Gen 5 drives are hotter, more expensive, and the real-world difference for architectural workloads is negligible.
or
Samsung 990 Pro
Similar pricing
Slightly faster in synthetic benchmarks, excellent reliability. Samsung or WD -- pick whichever is cheaper.
CPU Cooler
Thermalright Peerless Assassin 120 SE
~$35
Dual-tower air cooler. Competes with coolers costing three times as much. Handles the 265K and 9800X3D easily, adequate for the 285K and 9950X at stock. Check RAM clearance on your motherboard.
or
Noctua NH-D15
~$100
The standard. Quieter than the Peerless Assassin, slightly better thermals. If you want a silent office machine, this is the air cooler. Chromax.black version available.
or if you want liquid
Arctic Liquid Freezer III 360
~$100
Best value AIO. Excellent cooling for the 285K and 9950X under sustained all-core loads. The 280mm fits the Fractal North; the 360mm fits the North XL and ProArt PA602 top mount. Air cooling is simpler -- get an AIO if you run sustained CPU renders or if silence under load matters.
Power Supply
Seasonic Focus GX-850
~$120
80+ Gold, fully modular, 10-year warranty. 850W is enough for any build below a 5090.
or
be quiet! Straight Power 12 850W
~$140
80+ Platinum, fully modular, very quiet. Slightly better efficiency, noticeably quieter under load.
or
Corsair RM850x (2024)
~$130
80+ Gold, fully modular, zero-RPM fan mode at low loads. Another safe choice.
If you are running a 5090: Step up to 1000W. The 5090 has 575W TDP with transient spikes above that. The Seasonic Focus GX-1000 (~$160), be quiet! Straight Power 12 1000W (~$180), or Corsair RM1000x (~$170).
The Build
Building a PC takes about 8 hours start to finish for someone who has never done it -- that includes watching a build guide, unboxing, assembly, BIOS setup, Windows install, and driver installation. The savings over a Puget Systems or BOXX prebuilt are $700--$1,500 depending on the spec. Whether that is worth your time depends on your billing rate and whether you enjoy it.
